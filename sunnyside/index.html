Detailed Termite Control Services

Our termite control service is designed to be comprehensive, addressing the problem from every angle. We don't just treat the visible signs of termites; we work to eliminate the colony and prevent future infestations. Our approach is thorough and tailored to the specific needs of your property.

Comprehensive Termite Inspections

The first step in effective termite control is a detailed inspection. Our trained technicians will meticulously examine all areas of your property, both inside and out, to identify signs of termite activity, determine the extent of the infestation, and locate entry points. This includes:

Examining Foundation and Exterior Walls

We look for mud tubes, which are a sure sign of subterranean termites. These tubes act as protected pathways for termites to travel from their underground colony to your home. We also check for any damaged wood, cracks in the foundation, or other potential entry points.

Inspecting Interior Areas

Inside your home, we inspect walls, ceilings, floors, attics, and crawl spaces. We look for signs like hollow-sounding wood, peeling paint that might indicate moisture damage (which attracts termites), and visible termite swarmers or discarded wings. We also check around windows, doors, and plumbing fixtures.

Analyzing Wood Structures

We carefully inspect all wood structures, including beams, joists, subfloors, and any other timber components. We tap on wood to check for hollowing and visually inspect for any damage caused by termites.

Targeted Termite Treatments

Based on the findings of our inspection, we will develop a customized treatment plan. We utilize a variety of advanced treatment methods to ensure the most effective and long-lasting results.

Liquid Termiticide Barriers

One of the most common and effective methods for subterranean termites is the application of a liquid termiticide around the perimeter of your home. This creates a protective barrier that termites cannot cross. When termites attempt to pass through the treated soil, they either die or carry the termiticide back to the colony, leading to its eventual elimination.

Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems involve placing bait stations around your property. These stations contain a slow-acting termiticide that termites consume and carry back to the colony, effectively poisoning the entire colony. Baiting systems are an excellent option for monitoring termite activity and providing long-term control.

Localized Treatments

For specific areas of infestation or certain types of termites, we may use localized treatments. This can include direct application of termiticides to infested wood or using foam termiticides to reach difficult-to-access areas. Our goal is to eliminate the active termites at the source.

Termite Prevention Strategies

Once the current infestation is under control, we focus on preventing future problems. Termite prevention is a crucial part of protecting your investment.

Moisture Control

Termites are attracted to moisture. We may recommend and help implement strategies to reduce moisture around your property, such as improving drainage, fixing leaky pipes, and ensuring proper ventilation in crawl spaces.

Exclusion Techniques

We identify and seal potential entry points that termites might use to access your home. This can involve repairing cracks in foundations, sealing gaps around pipes and wires, and ensuring proper screening on vents.

Regular Inspections and Monitoring

We offer ongoing inspection and monitoring services to detect any new termite activity early on. Regular check-ups provide peace of mind and help ensure that your property remains protected.

Common Termite Problems and How We Solve Them

Understanding the signs of a termite infestation is crucial for early detection and intervention. Many homeowners overlook early indicators, which allows termites to cause significant and costly damage. Here are some common problems associated with termites and how our services provide the solution.

Visible Termite Swarmers

Termite swarmers, also known as alates, are the reproductive members of a termite colony. When a colony reaches a certain size and maturity, swarmers emerge to reproduce and establish new colonies. Seeing swarmers in or around your home, especially in springtime, is a clear sign of an active termite infestation. Discarded wings near windowsills or light sources are also a strong indicator that a termite swarm has recently occurred.

Our Solution: If you see swarmers, contact us immediately. While the swarmers themselves are relatively harmless, their presence signifies an established colony nearby. Our inspection will confirm the presence of termites, and our targeted treatments will eliminate the existing colony and prevent further swarming in the future. We will also identify where the swarmers were emerging from to address the source of the infestation.

Mud Tubes on Walls or Foundations

Subterranean termites build mud tubes to travel between their underground colonies and their food source – your home's wooden structure. These tubes are typically made of soil, wood, and termite saliva and can be found on exterior walls, foundations, plumbing pipes, or floor joists. They provide termites with protection from predators and the elements as they move.

Our Solution: Finding mud tubes is a definitive sign of subterranean termites. Our technicians will carefully follow these tubes to locate the source of the infestation within your structure. Our liquid termiticide barrier treatment effectively eliminates termites as they attempt to use these tubes, disrupting their pathways and ultimately leading to the demise of the colony. For ongoing protection, we may also recommend monitoring with baiting systems.

Hollow-Sounding Wood

Termites consume wood from the inside out, leaving a thin veneer of wood or paint on the surface. As they eat away the interior, the wood becomes hollow. Tapping on suspected areas with a screwdriver handle can reveal this hollow sound. This type of damage often goes unnoticed until it is significant, as the exterior of the wood may appear undamaged.

Our Solution: Hollow-sounding wood indicates that termites have been actively feeding within the structure. Our detailed inspection will pinpoint the extent of this damage. We will then implement localized treatments to eliminate the termites within the damaged areas and apply preventative measures to protect surrounding wood. Depending on the severity of the damage, we can also advise on necessary repairs or structural reinforcement.

Damaged Wood Structures

While termites eat wood from the inside, severe infestations can lead to visible damage on the exterior of wooden structures. This can include sagging floors, buckling walls, doors and windows that stick, and blistered or peeling paint. The damage caused by termites can compromise the structural integrity of your home.

Our Solution: Visible damage is a serious indicator of a significant termite infestation. Our team will assess the full extent of the structural damage caused by termites. Our advanced treatment methods are designed to eliminate the termites quickly and thoroughly, preventing further destruction. While termite control is our primary focus, we can also recommend qualified contractors in Sunnyside for any necessary repairs or structural remediation.

Small Piles of Fecal Pellets (Frass)

Drywood termites, unlike subterranean termites, live entirely within the wood they infest. They don't build mud tubes. Instead, they create kick-out holes in the wood and push out small piles of fecal pellets, which resemble sawdust or sand. These tiny pellets, known as frass, can often be found beneath infested wood.

Our Solution: Frass is a clear sign of a drywood termite infestation. Our treatment methods for drywood termites often involve localized treatments, such as injecting termiticides directly into the galleries within the infested wood. We may also use spot treatments and, in some cases, fumigation for widespread drywood termite issues, although this is less common in our immediate service area. Our inspection will determine the most effective treatment for your specific drywood termite problem.

Tight-Fitting Doors and Windows

Wooden doors and windows that suddenly become difficult to open or close can be an indication of termites infesting the wooden frames. As termites tunnel through the wood, it can warp and swell, causing these operational issues. While moisture is a common cause of this, combined with other termite signs, it's worth investigating.

Our Solution: If tight-fitting doors and windows are accompanied by other signs of termite activity, it's likely due to an infestation in the frames. Our inspection will confirm if termites are the cause. We will then apply targeted treatments to the infested areas to eliminate the termites and prevent further damage to the wooden frames. Addressing the infestation will often alleviate the issues with the doors and windows.

Discovering Termite Wings

After a termite swarm, the reproductive swarmers shed their wings. Finding small, discarded wings, often clustered together, near windowsills, light fixtures, or in spiderwebs, is a strong indicator that a termite swarm has happened inside or very close to your property. These wings are fragile and often break off easily.

Our Solution: The presence of termite wings confirms that a mature termite colony is present and has recently swarmed. This means an infestation is active. Our inspection will locate the source of the swarm and the extent of the colony. Our comprehensive treatment plan will eliminate the existing colony, reducing the risk of new colonies being established nearby and preventing future swarming within your property.

Termite Galleries in Damaged Wood

When you break open wood that has been damaged by termites, you will see intricate tunnels and galleries carved out by the termites. These galleries often follow the grain of the wood, and they can be packed with mud or hollow, depending on the type of termite. Discovering these galleries is definitive proof of a termite infestation.

Our Solution: Finding termite galleries during an inspection or accidentally broken wood confirms an active and likely significant infestation. Our detailed inspection will determine the extent of the gallery system and the overall infestation. We will then apply our targeted treatments, such as liquid termiticides or localized applications, to infiltrate these galleries, eliminate the termites within, and prevent further tunneling and damage.
